Display Ads

What Are They?

Display ads are those banners, boxes, or videos you see on websites. Ad networks like Google AdSense pay you every time someone views or clicks them. It’s a simple way to earn passive income, and you’ve probably seen them all over the internet.

How Do They Work?

You sign up with an ad network, paste their code into your site, and voilà—ads start showing up. You get paid based on impressions (how many people see the ad) or clicks (how many people interact with it).

Pros and Cons

  • Pros: Easy to set up, scales with traffic.
  • Cons: Can annoy users if overdone, lower earnings without huge traffic.

Best Practices

  • Place ads where they’re visible but not intrusive (like the side of the page).
  • Avoid covering content—users hate that.
  • Test different sizes and positions to see what works.

Affiliate Marketing

What Is It?

Affiliate marketing is when you promote someone else’s product and earn a commission for every sale made through your link. Think of it like being a digital salesperson.

How Does It Work?

You join an affiliate program (like Amazon Associates), grab a unique link, and share it on your site. When someone buys through your link, you get a cut—usually 5-20% of the sale.

Pros and Cons

  • Pros: High earning potential, no inventory needed.
  • Cons: Takes time to build trust, depends on conversions.

Best Practices

  • Pick products that match your audience.
  • Be honest—disclose that it’s an affiliate link.
  • Use compelling calls‑to‑action (CTAs).

Sponsored Content

What Is It?

Sponsored content is when a brand pays you to write about them or feature their stuff on your site. It’s like an ad, but more integrated into your content.

How Does It Work?

A company contacts you (or you pitch them), you agree on a fee, and you create a post or section showcasing their product. Disclosure is key—it’s legally required!

Pros and Cons

  • Pros: Big payouts, builds partnerships.
  • Cons: Requires an audience, can feel salesy.

Best Practices

  • Keep it authentic—only promote what you’d use.
  • Label it clearly: “Sponsored by [Brand].”
  • Negotiate fair rates based on your reach.

Selling Digital Products

What Is It?

You create and sell your own stuff—like ebooks, courses, or templates—directly to your audience. It’s all profit, minus platform fees.

How Does It Work?

Make a product, host it on a platform like Gumroad or Teachable, and link to it from your site. People buy, you deliver digitally.

Pros and Cons

  • Pros: High margins, full control.
  • Cons: Takes time to create, needs marketing.

Best Practices

  • Solve a problem your audience has.
  • Price it right—test $5, $10, or $20.
  • Offer a sample to hook buyers.

Memberships & Subscriptions

What Is It?

You charge visitors for access to premium content—like exclusive articles, videos, or a community. Think Patreon or Substack.

How Does It Work?

Set up a paywall using a tool like Memberful, then offer teasers to entice sign‑ups. Charge monthly or yearly.

Pros and Cons

  • Pros: Recurring revenue, loyal fans.
  • Cons: Need consistent value, harder to start.

Best Practices

  • Give freebies to build trust.
  • Keep premium content fresh.
  • Start small—$5/month is a sweet spot.

Donations

What Is It?

You ask your audience to support you directly, usually through platforms like Buy Me a Coffee or PayPal.

How Does It Work?

Add a donation button or link, and encourage contributions with a heartfelt message. It’s all voluntary!

Pros and Cons

  • Pros: Simple, no middleman.
  • Cons: Unpredictable, relies on generosity.

Best Practices

  • Say “thank you” sincerely.
  • Offer perks (e.g., a shoutout).
  • Don’t guilt‑trip your readers.

Advanced Techniques

What Are They?

Once you’ve mastered the basics, level up with these advanced methods:

  • Programmatic Ads: Automated ad buying for better rates.
  • Native Advertising: Ads that blend into your content, like a chameleon.

How Do They Work?

Programmatic uses AI to sell ad space; native ads match your site’s style for higher engagement.

Quick Tips

  • Research ad exchanges for programmatic.
  • Design native ads to feel organic.
